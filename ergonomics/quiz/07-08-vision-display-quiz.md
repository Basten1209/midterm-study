# Vision / Visual Display Design Quiz

Source: 05_Vision / Visual Display Design_Part I / Part II

## Question 1
According to the anatomy and physiology of the human eye, which photoreceptors are responsible for daylight vision and color perception?

A. Cornea
B. Cones
C. Fovea centralis
D. Rods

Answer: B. Cones

## Question 2
When a person looks into an empty visual field or total darkness, the eye's resting state of accommodation naturally reverts to an intermediate distance of about 1 meter. What is this phenomenon called?

A. Light adaptation
B. Far point
C. Dark focus
D. Visual angle

Answer: C. Dark focus

## Question 3
Which typographical criterion specifically refers to the quality of alphanumeric characters that makes it possible for each individual character to be identifiable from others?

A. Legibility
B. Readability
C. Comprehension
D. Visibility

Answer: A. Legibility

## Question 4
Reading speed from older, low-resolution VDTs is typically 20 to 30 percent slower than reading from hardcopy text. What is a primary reason for this?

A. The dot-matrix image quality and stimulation of spatial frequency channels.
B. The overuse of 9 o'clock pointer alignments.
C. The dark focus effect of the screen.
D. The use of highly saturated red and blue colors.

Answer: A. The dot-matrix image quality and stimulation of spatial frequency channels.

## Question 5
When designing a VDT interface, under what specific circumstance should blinking text or highlighting be utilized?

A. To highlight routine operational status updates.
B. To create an emergent feature.
C. To indicate highly critical warnings or urgent emergencies.
D. To organize data into clear vertical columns.

Answer: C. To indicate highly critical warnings or urgent emergencies.

## Question 6
Why do human factors guidelines recommend using simple 2D representations rather than 3D blocks in data graphs?

A. 2D representations use different flash rates to convey data.
B. 3D blocks cause a "volume illusion" where users perceive the overall volume instead of just the height.
C. 3D blocks require more ink to print.
D. 2D representations stimulate the rods rather than the cones.

Answer: B. 3D blocks cause a "volume illusion" where users perceive the overall volume instead of just the height.

## Question 7
Which psychometric tool is commonly used to measure a user's preference or attitude toward a graphic interface by asking them to rate their level of agreement (e.g., Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)?

A. Flesch Reading Scale
B. Likert Scale
C. Snellen Scale
D. Gestalt Scale

Answer: B. Likert Scale

## Question 8
According to guidelines for quantitative displays, in which scenario is a digital display superior to an analog display?

A. When checking if a system is within a normal safety range.
B. When the exact rate of change needs to be observed.
C. When predicting the future direction of an aircraft.
D. When a precise numeric value is required and values remain stable enough to be read.

Answer: D. When a precise numeric value is required and values remain stable enough to be read.

## Question 9
Which specific type of analog display is known to be prone to "reversal errors" and makes it harder for operators to visualize the entire scale range?

A. Fixed scale with moving pointer
B. Object display
C. Moving scale with fixed pointer
D. Digital counter

Answer: C. Moving scale with fixed pointer

## Question 10
Wickens' Compatibility of Proximity Principle asserts that high display proximity, meaning grouping elements physically or perceptually, is most beneficial for which type of task?

A. Focused attention tasks
B. Mental integration tasks
C. Visual acuity tasks
D. Check reading tasks

Answer: B. Mental integration tasks

## Question 11
When multiple variables are mapped to a geometric object, like a polygon, a new perceptual property appears, such as a specific overall shape or area, that does not exist in the individual parts. What is this property called?

A. Emergent feature
B. Chromostereopsis
C. Parallax effect
D. Pictorial realism

Answer: A. Emergent feature

## Question 12
When arranging multiple dials on a panel for check reading, why is it recommended to align all pointers to the 9 o'clock position for normal status?

A. A single deviant pointer breaks the pattern and "pops out" pre-attentively.
B. It reduces the parallax error in reading.
C. It creates an emergent feature of volume.
D. It provides precise quantitative values for every dial.

Answer: A. A single deviant pointer breaks the pattern and "pops out" pre-attentively.

## Question 13
How does applying color coding (e.g., dividing a scale into green, yellow, and red zones) improve a qualitative display?

A. It converts a moving-scale display into a fixed-scale display.
B. It improves the Snellen acuity of the operator.
C. It reduces cognitive processing time by instantly signifying system status (Normal, Caution, Danger).
D. It eliminates the need for a physical pointer.

Answer: C. It reduces cognitive processing time by instantly signifying system status (Normal, Caution, Danger).

## Question 14
In the context of monitoring complex systems, what are the three sequential levels of Situation Awareness (SA)?

A. Perception, Comprehension, Projection
B. Searching, Identifying, Tracking
C. Alerting, Diagnosis, Intervention
D. Visibility, Legibility, Readability

Answer: A. Perception, Comprehension, Projection

## Question 15
Which of Roscoe's principles states that the movement depicted on an aircraft display should be congruent with the operator's frame of reference (e.g., an outside-in moving-aircraft display with a fixed horizon)?

A. Principle of Integration
B. Principle of Pursuit Presentation
C. Principle of Pictorial Realism
D. Principle of Compatible Motion

Answer: D. Principle of Compatible Motion
