# Audition / Speech Communication Quiz

Source: 06_Audition / Speech Communication_Part I / Part II

## Question 1
If the sound power of a noise source is doubled, how does this change affect the sound-pressure level (SPL)?

A. It increases the SPL by exactly 3 dB.
B. It increases the SPL by 10 dB.
C. It doubles the SPL.
D. It increases the SPL by exactly 6 dB.

Answer: A. It increases the SPL by exactly 3 dB.

## Question 2
Which of the following statements best describes the relationship between frequency and the human perception of pitch?

A. Tones between 1000 and 3000 Hz are highly sensitive to intensity-induced pitch changes.
B. Low-frequency tones always sound higher in pitch as intensity increases.
C. Pitch is a subjective psychological sensation that can be influenced by sound intensity.
D. Pitch is a direct physical measurement identical to frequency.

Answer: C. Pitch is a subjective psychological sensation that can be influenced by sound intensity.

## Question 3
How does the middle ear amplify sound pressure before it reaches the fluid-filled inner ear?

A. Through the tonotopic vibration of the basilar membrane.
B. By the lever action of the ossicles and the large surface area of the tympanic membrane compared to the oval window.
C. By utilizing the acoustic reflex of the stapedius muscle.
D. Through the resonant properties of the auditory canal.

Answer: B. By the lever action of the ossicles and the large surface area of the tympanic membrane compared to the oval window.

## Question 4
Which theory of hearing best explains the human perception of low-frequency tones (below 1000 Hz)?

A. Conductive theory
B. Place theory
C. Temporal theory
D. Auditory masking theory

Answer: C. Temporal theory

## Question 5
To ensure a signal is reliably detected in a noisy environment without causing annoyance or startle, how far above the masked threshold should the signal intensity typically be set?

A. 40 to 50 dB
B. 15 to 25 dB
C. 0 to 5 dB
D. At least 60 dB

Answer: B. 15 to 25 dB

## Question 6
Why is it dangerous to design a system that requires an operator to absolutely identify many different pure tones from memory?

A. Because pure tones take too long (over 2 seconds) to build up in the ear.
B. Because pure tones cannot be effectively masked by background noise.
C. Because humans can only reliably identify about 4 to 7 levels of frequency on an absolute basis.
D. Because frequency discrimination is entirely independent of sound intensity.

Answer: C. Because humans can only reliably identify about 4 to 7 levels of frequency on an absolute basis.

## Question 7
The design principle of "approximation" suggests that complex auditory information should be presented using:

A. A continuously varying frequency to prevent adaptation.
B. A spatial sound source that moves around the operator.
C. A two-stage signal consisting of an attention-demanding signal followed by a precise designation signal.
D. A single, high-intensity blast of sound.

Answer: C. A two-stage signal consisting of an attention-demanding signal followed by a precise designation signal.

## Question 8
The principle of "dissociability" in auditory display design dictates that:

A. Systems should provide minimal information to save cognitive load.
B. Auditory signals must be easily discernible from ongoing ambient noise or other display sounds.
C. The same signal should consistently represent the same event.
D. Signals should use natural relationships, like a high pitch for "up".

Answer: B. Auditory signals must be easily discernible from ongoing ambient noise or other display sounds.

## Question 9
What is the generally recommended frequency range for warning alarms to take advantage of the ear's sensitivity and ensure detectability?

A. Below 500 Hz
B. 500 to 3000 Hz
C. Above 10,000 Hz
D. 4000 to 8000 Hz

Answer: B. 500 to 3000 Hz

## Question 10
Which of the following correctly describes the relationship between vowels and consonants in speech communication?

A. Both vowels and consonants contribute equally to speech power and intelligibility.
B. Vowels possess much greater speech power, but the lower-intensity consonants carry the critical cues for intelligibility.
C. Consonants have higher acoustic power and provide the physical volume of speech.
D. Vowels have lower acoustic power but are more important for intelligibility than consonants.

Answer: B. Vowels possess much greater speech power, but the lower-intensity consonants carry the critical cues for intelligibility.

## Question 11
When evaluating a speech communication system, how does "speech intelligibility" differ from "speech quality"?

A. Intelligibility only applies to synthesized speech, while quality applies to natural speech.
B. Intelligibility refers to the naturalness of the voice, while quality refers to its loudness.
C. Intelligibility is the degree to which the message is correctly recognized, while quality refers to the naturalness and speaker recognizability.
D. There is no difference; they are synonymous terms in human factors engineering.

Answer: C. Intelligibility is the degree to which the message is correctly recognized, while quality refers to the naturalness and speaker recognizability.

## Question 12
How can a designer improve speech intelligibility in a noisy environment using message characteristics?

A. By using an infinitely large vocabulary.
B. By presenting words in isolation rather than in sentences.
C. By using short words and single letters instead of the phonetic alphabet.
D. By restricting the vocabulary size and arranging words into predictable, meaningful sentences.

Answer: D. By restricting the vocabulary size and arranging words into predictable, meaningful sentences.

## Question 13
What is the primary purpose of the Articulation Index (AI)?

A. To measure the exact reverberation time of an enclosed room.
B. To directly measure the absolute identification limit of pure tones.
C. To determine the maximum vocal effort required for a speaker.
D. To predict speech intelligibility by calculating the weighted signal-to-noise ratios across different frequency bands.

Answer: D. To predict speech intelligibility by calculating the weighted signal-to-noise ratios across different frequency bands.

## Question 14
What is a major disadvantage of the "synthesis by analysis" method for generating artificial speech?

A. It requires mathematical rules to generate all speech sounds.
B. It demands an incredibly low amount of computer memory compared to synthesis by rule.
C. It can easily generate an infinite vocabulary without human recordings.
D. It lacks natural coarticulation, making words sound awkward and choppy when linked together into sentences.

Answer: D. It lacks natural coarticulation, making words sound awkward and choppy when linked together into sentences.

## Question 15
According to human factors guidelines for synthesized speech, what is an effective strategy to improve a user's ability to recall a synthesized voice message?

A. Precede every spoken message with a loud, startle-inducing tone.
B. Use center clipping to enhance the consonant sounds.
C. Increase the interword intervals (pauses) to give the listener more time to process the information.
D. Speed up the presentation rate to minimize exposure time.

Answer: C. Increase the interword intervals (pauses) to give the listener more time to process the information.
