# Intro to HF/E Part 1 Quiz

Source: 02_Introduction to HFE_Part I

## Question 1
What is the literal Greek etymology of the word "Ergonomics"?

A. Human + Measurement
B. Work + Laws
C. Body + Design
D. Skill + Study

Answer: B. Work + Laws

## Question 2
Which of the following is the primary philosophy of HF/E?

A. Fitting the system/task to the human
B. Selecting only the most capable workers
C. Forcing humans to adapt to technology
D. Maximizing machine speed at all costs

Answer: A. Fitting the system/task to the human

## Question 3
Which domain of ergonomics focuses on mental processes like perception and memory?

A. Neuroergonomics
B. Organizational Ergonomics
C. Cognitive Ergonomics
D. Physical Ergonomics

Answer: C. Cognitive Ergonomics

## Question 4
What were the 17 fundamental motions identified by the Gilbreths called?

A. Therbligs
B. Ergos
C. Nomos
D. Taylors

Answer: A. Therbligs

## Question 5
How did the focus of behavioral scientists change after World War II?

A. Shifting from "fitting the human to the system" to "fitting the system to the human"
B. Developing the first handheld computers
C. Maximizing agricultural output
D. Designing simple kitchen utensils

Answer: A. Shifting from "fitting the human to the system" to "fitting the system to the human"

## Question 6
Alphonse Chapanis (1983) argued that improving one HF/E objective often:

A. Compromises all other objectives
B. Increases the cost exponentially
C. Enhances multiple other objectives simultaneously
D. Requires specialized training for users

Answer: C. Enhances multiple other objectives simultaneously

## Question 7
Which level of ergonomic design focuses on anticipating issues during the design phase or prototype evaluation to avoid future problems?

A. Administrative
B. Creative
C. Corrective (Reactive)
D. Preventative (Proactive)

Answer: D. Preventative (Proactive)

## Question 8
Why is using "oneself as the model" insufficient for design?

A. It fails to account for diverse user variability and anthropometric diversity
B. Designers are always more capable than users
C. Most designers lack "common sense"
D. It is too expensive

Answer: A. It fails to account for diverse user variability and anthropometric diversity

## Question 9
According to Fitts' Law and the Speed-Accuracy Trade-off:

A. Accuracy is independent of response speed
B. Increasing speed always increases accuracy
C. Faster responses typically lead to higher error rates
D. Task difficulty has no effect on error rates

Answer: C. Faster responses typically lead to higher error rates

## Question 10
The "Iceberg Effect" of ergonomic costs means that:

A. Safety is only important in cold climates
B. Indirect costs (hidden) are often 4-10 times higher than direct medical costs
C. Companies should ignore minor injuries
D. Most injuries are visible and easy to calculate

Answer: B. Indirect costs (hidden) are often 4-10 times higher than direct medical costs

## Question 11
What are the four basic functions performed in a human-machine system?

A. Power, Control, Display, Feedback
B. Input, Processing, Output, Storage
C. Sensing, Information Storage, Processing & Decision, Action
D. Design, Build, Test, Deploy

Answer: C. Sensing, Information Storage, Processing & Decision, Action

## Question 12
Does automation eliminate the need for human factors?

A. Yes, because machines make no errors.
B. No, it shifts the human role to monitoring and exception handling.
C. Yes, because the software is programmed.
D. Yes, because human factors is needed only in manufacturing systems.

Answer: B. No, it shifts the human role to monitoring and exception handling.

## Question 13
In a parallel system, the overall reliability (Rsystem) is:

A. Equal to the weakest component
B. Higher than any single component because of redundancy
C. Always 100%
D. Lower than the reliability of a single component

Answer: B. Higher than any single component because of redundancy

## Question 14
Which standard is the "Department of Defense Design Criteria for Human Engineering"?

A. IEC 62366
B. MIL-STD-1472H
C. ISO 6385
D. ANSI/HFES 100

Answer: B. MIL-STD-1472H

## Question 15
Why is a "participatory approach" critical for new working methods?

A. It eliminates the need for experts
B. It always results in the cheapest equipment
C. It builds employee trust and reduces resistance to change
D. It is faster than top-down design

Answer: C. It builds employee trust and reduces resistance to change
