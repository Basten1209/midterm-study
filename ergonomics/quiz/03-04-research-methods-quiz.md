# Research Methods in HF/E Quiz

Source: 03_Research Methods in HFE_Part I / Part II

## Question 1
What is the primary purpose of conducting Human Factors/Ergonomics (HF/E) research?

A. To systematically collect empirical data to optimize human well-being and system performance
B. To train human operators to adapt to poorly designed machinery
C. To eliminate the need for human operators in automated systems
D. To rely on the designer's intuition for interface layout

Answer: A. To systematically collect empirical data to optimize human well-being and system performance

## Question 2
In descriptive research, variables that are used to group or categorize the population (e.g., age, gender, experience level) are known as:

A. Confounding variables
B. Dependent variables
C. Criterion variables
D. Stratification variables

Answer: D. Stratification variables

## Question 3
Which type of evaluation is conducted early in the design process to identify problems and guide iterative improvements?

A. Summative evaluation
B. Formative evaluation
C. Descriptive evaluation
D. Construct evaluation

Answer: B. Formative evaluation

## Question 4
In the same car dashboard experiment, the researcher measures the driver's reaction time to an alert. The reaction time is the:

A. Independent Variable (IV)
B. Dependent Variable (DV)
C. Control Variable
D. Confounding Variable

Answer: B. Dependent Variable (DV)

## Question 5
What is a confounding variable in experimental research?

A. The variable the researcher wants to measure
B. The baseline measurement taken before the experiment begins
C. An uncontrolled "noise" factor that can unintentionally affect the dependent variable
D. A factor that is intentionally manipulated to test a hypothesis

Answer: C. An uncontrolled "noise" factor that can unintentionally affect the dependent variable

## Question 6
An experimental design where each participant experiences only one condition (e.g., Group A uses Keyboard A, Group B uses Keyboard B) is called:

A. Between-subjects design
B. Within-subjects design
C. Longitudinal design
D. Repeated measures design

Answer: A. Between-subjects design

## Question 7
"Ecological validity" refers to:

A. How environmentally friendly the system is
B. The consistency of the measurement tools used
C. The degree to which research findings can be generalized to real-world, natural settings
D. The statistical power of the research design

Answer: C. The degree to which research findings can be generalized to real-world, natural settings

## Question 8
Why is it critical to use a representative sample in HF/E research?

A. It guarantees that the results will be statistically significant
B. It ensures the findings accurately reflect the target user population and promotes inclusive design
C. It reduces the cost of the experiment
D. It eliminates the need to control extraneous variables

Answer: B. It ensures the findings accurately reflect the target user population and promotes inclusive design

## Question 9
What is the relationship between effect size and the required sample size?

A. As effect size decreases, the required sample size increases
B. Effect size has no impact on the required sample size
C. A smaller effect size means a smaller sample is needed
D. As effect size increases, the required sample size increases

Answer: A. As effect size decreases, the required sample size increases

## Question 10
In psychometric requirements, "Reliability" refers to:

A. The degree to which the test measures what it claims to measure
B. The ability of the tool to detect tiny changes in human performance
C. The cost-effectiveness of the measurement tool
D. The consistency, stability, and repeatability of a measurement

Answer: D. The consistency, stability, and repeatability of a measurement

## Question 11
If a cognitive workload questionnaire covers all relevant dimensions of workload (mental, physical, temporal, frustration), it is said to have strong:

A. Construct validity
B. Ecological validity
C. Face validity
D. Content validity

Answer: D. Content validity

## Question 12
The degree to which a measurement tool accurately reflects an abstract theoretical concept (e.g., "situational awareness" or "mental stress") is known as:

A. Face validity
B. Construct validity
C. Content validity
D. System-descriptive validity

Answer: B. Construct validity

## Question 13
A performance metric is "free from contamination" when:

A. It is cheap to collect
B. It relies entirely on subjective feedback
C. The participant knows the exact purpose of the study
D. It measures exactly what the researcher intended, without being affected by irrelevant extraneous variables

Answer: D. It measures exactly what the researcher intended, without being affected by irrelevant extraneous variables

## Question 14
To compare the mean task completion times between four different website layouts, the most appropriate statistical technique is:

A. Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)
B. Correlation analysis
C. Homogeneity chi-square test
D. Independent samples t-test

Answer: A. Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

## Question 15
A new software update reduces task time by 0.01 seconds. The result has a p-value of 0.02 (statistically significant), but the update costs $5 million to implement. This highlights the difference between:

A. Laboratory and field settings
B. Descriptive and inferential statistics
C. Statistical significance and practical significance
D. Reliability and validity

Answer: C. Statistical significance and practical significance
