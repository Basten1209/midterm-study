# Information Processing Quiz

Source: 04_Information Processing_Part I / Part II

## Question 1
According to Information Theory, how is "Information" (H) formally defined?

A. The physical importance of a warning signal
B. The total number of symbols in a message
C. The speed at which a stimulus is perceived
D. The reduction of uncertainty upon the occurrence of an event

Answer: D. The reduction of uncertainty upon the occurrence of an event

## Question 2
The Hick-Hyman Law suggests that as the amount of stimulus information (H) increases, the choice reaction time (RT) will:

A. Remain constant regardless of bits
B. Decrease exponentially
C. Increase linearly
D. Increase as a power function

Answer: C. Increase linearly

## Question 3
According to ergonomic guidelines, when should a designer prioritize an auditory presentation over a visual one?

A. When the message calls for immediate action
B. When the message will be referred to later
C. When the information deals with location in space
D. When the message is long and complex

Answer: A. When the message calls for immediate action

## Question 4
In a multimodal safety strategy, what is the recommended sequence for presenting information?

A. Auditory for alerting, then visual for diagnosis
B. Use only one modality to avoid sensory overload
C. Visual for alerting, then auditory for diagnosis
D. Simultaneous use of both for all types of messages

Answer: A. Auditory for alerting, then visual for diagnosis

## Question 5
"Absolute Judgment" differs from "Relative Judgment" because it requires the user to:

A. Detect the presence of a signal against a noisy background
B. Compare two stimuli presented side-by-side
C. Rank stimuli from highest to lowest
D. Identify a stimulus based solely on their memory of a standard

Answer: D. Identify a stimulus based solely on their memory of a standard

## Question 6
Miller's "Magical Number 7 +/- 2" refers to the typical range of:

A. Seconds that iconic memory can hold an image
B. Relative discriminations a person can make
C. Items that can be identified on an absolute basis along a single dimension
D. Bits of information the brain can process per second

Answer: C. Items that can be identified on an absolute basis along a single dimension

## Question 7
In Signal Detection Theory (SDT), the parameter d' (d-prime) represents:

A. The cost of a Miss
B. The probability of a False Alarm
C. The sensitivity or discriminability of the sensory system
D. The observer's decision bias or strategy

Answer: C. The sensitivity or discriminability of the sensory system

## Question 8
If the cost of a "Miss" is extremely high (e.g., missing a security threat), the observer is likely to shift their criterion beta to the:

A. Top, to increase sensitivity
B. Left, becoming more "risky" to ensure more Hits
C. Right, becoming more conservative
D. Center, to balance Hits and False Alarms

Answer: B. Left, becoming more "risky" to ensure more Hits

## Question 9
Which memory subsystem acts as the "gateway" where information must be attended to before entering long-term storage?

A. Working Memory
B. Echoic Storage
C. Iconic Storage
D. Semantic Store

Answer: A. Working Memory

## Question 10
"Chunking" is a technique used to effectively increase the capacity of Working Memory by:

A. Converting visual codes into phonetic codes
B. Grouping individual items into familiar, meaningful units
C. Reducing the rate of information transmission
D. Increasing the duration of sensory storage

Answer: B. Grouping individual items into familiar, meaningful units

## Question 11
"Vigilance Decrement" refers to the decline in detection speed and accuracy that typically occurs after:

A. 20 to 35 minutes of a task
B. 5 minutes of a task
C. Only when the person is sleep-deprived
D. 2 hours of a task

Answer: A. 20 to 35 minutes of a task

## Question 12
According to Multiple-Resource Theory, time-sharing between two tasks is most efficient when the tasks:

A. Use the same processing codes (e.g., both verbal)
B. Utilize different resource pools (e.g., one spatial, one verbal)
C. Demand resources from the same input modality (e.g., both visual)

Answer: B. Utilize different resource pools (e.g., one spatial, one verbal)

## Question 13
Which type of compatibility deals with the degree to which symbols correspond to a person's conceptual associations (e.g., an airplane icon for an airport)?

A. Modality Compatibility
B. Movement Compatibility
C. Spatial Compatibility
D. Conceptual Compatibility

Answer: D. Conceptual Compatibility

## Question 14
As people age (typically after 65), which change is most commonly observed in their information processing capabilities?

A. Improved ability to handle conceptual incompatibility
B. Enhanced capacity for time-sharing multiple tasks
C. Increased sensitivity (d') to weak signals
D. A slowing of performance in retrieving and executing responses

Answer: D. A slowing of performance in retrieving and executing responses

## Question 15
The "Secondary Task Measure" of mental workload is based on the logic that:

A. Primary task performance is always the most sensitive measure
B. Physiological changes are the only objective way to measure load
C. Spare capacity not used by the primary task will be used by the secondary task
D. Mental workload is a single-dimensional concept

Answer: C. Spare capacity not used by the primary task will be used by the secondary task
